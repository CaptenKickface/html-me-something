Why do you go by Toby?

In High School

The name Toby initially came from high school. In my freshman year, I was hanging with some upper-classmen when they decided to come up with dumb nicknames. Things like Fred or Larry got passed around, but my nickname became Toby.

Roots

While everyone else's nickname faded away after a week; mine stuck because of a mini-series called "Roots". In it, there's an incredibly iconic scene that involves the use of the name Toby. In my school district this mini-series was required material in middle school, so everyone found it fun to continue calling me Toby. Ingrained Irony

At this point I had begun to introduce myself to people as Toby. After a bit, I did more research into what Toby really meant. Toby, or Tobias, means "praise god" in Hebrew. As a non-religious person that grew up with very catholic parents, the irony was not lost on me and I felt compelled to continue going by Toby.

Jutsu/Japanese

Around the same time I was changing my name, I also began training in martial arts; specifically Ninjutstu. While training, we also were taught Japanese to learn the names of the moves. Coincidentally, Toby in Japanese translates to jump/agility (also construction worker but that ruins my narrative); the parkour parts of our training were what I enjoyed most.

Pledge to Progress

At age 18 I developed a headache that would last for 3+ years. During that time I mentally worked through finding peace through the pain and created a sort of creed. This became a mantra for me to calm myself during excruciating moments. Spontaneously, I ended the mantra with "I am Tobias Sol", which I plan to now change my name to.

Friendly Face

Later in life I wanted to change my image. For most of it I had been an aggressive person and decided to now become an easy-going presence at work and at home. While working with kids at a daycare, I realized "Toby" really counteracted my resting b**** face and kids had fun simply saying my name. To be fair "Mr. Toby" sounds ridiculous.
